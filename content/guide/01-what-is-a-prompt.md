---
title: What is a prompt?
tier: basics
tags: [intro, fundamentals]
---

A **prompt** is just what you type to a model. It's instructions, a question, or both. The model reads your text and predicts the most likely next words.

You can picture it as sliding a note under the door of a very fast librarian:

- If the note is clear and specific, they fetch what you want.
- If the note is vague, they guess — and usually wrong.

A useful mental model: the prompt is the entire world the model sees. Everything outside of it (your intentions, the user's real goal, the system it runs inside) has to be encoded into text the model can read.

> good prompts are mostly about being specific, giving examples, and leaving as little to "interpretation" as possible.
