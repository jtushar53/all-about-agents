You've covered prompting. Next layer up is **agents** — systems that take actions, call tools, and loop.

A taste of what's ahead:

- **Tool use** — teach the model to call functions (search, calculator, code exec). The prompt tells the model *what* tools exist and *when* to use them.
- **ReAct** — interleave reasoning ("Thought:") with actions ("Action:"). The loop feeds the observation back in.
- **Reflexion / self-critique** — let the model grade its own output and try again.
- **Multi-agent** — specialized agents talk to each other (planner, critic, executor).
- **DSPy / programmatic prompting** — treat prompts as compiled artifacts from optimization loops, not hand-written text.

The library page has deeper entries on each. The search scene lets you dive into papers from the open research literature.

Keep your eval harness. You'll need it more, not less, as systems get fancier.
