title: Clarity and specificity
tier: basics
tags: [clarity, specificity]
---

Compare:

- "Write something about dogs."
- "Write a 150-word friendly paragraph for a children's book introducing a golden retriever named Biscuit, ages 5-7, avoid scary content."

The second prompt hides nothing: audience, length, tone, persona, constraints. That is not "over-engineering" — it's how you stop the model from guessing.

Two cheap wins:

- Replace vague adjectives ("good", "short", "nice") with measurable ones (word count, reading level, banned words).
- Swap open questions ("what do you think?") for concrete tasks ("list 3 options, then pick one and justify in one sentence").

A good reality check: could a human freelancer complete this with only your prompt, no follow-up? If not, add whatever's missing.
